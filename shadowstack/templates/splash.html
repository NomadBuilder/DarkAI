Destroying lives

AI pornography is not a trend. It’s a weapon.

People are being digitally violated without warning. Their faces are stolen. Their bodies are fabricated. Their identities are twisted into something they never consented to — something that can follow them for years.

These images do not simply “go viral.” They are copied, mirrored, and monetized across a network of sites that hide behind layers of technical infrastructure. Victims are left to fight a system they can’t even see.

Our mission is simple and urgent: expose the systems that enable this harm, so that advocates, regulators, and platforms have a clear map of where pressure and policy must be applied.

What the tool actually does

The NCII Infrastructure Mapping Tool ingests URLs of abusive websites and automatically gathers public infrastructure metadata: hosting providers, CDNs, name servers, payment processors, analytics tags, and CMS fingerprints.

The output is an interactive graph that shows clusters of service providers most frequently connected to harmful content — turning a chaotic landscape into an actionable map for investigations and policy work.
