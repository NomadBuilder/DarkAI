Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about Dark AI's investigative tools and research reports.

Platform Overview

What is Dark AI?

Dark AI is a platform of investigative tools designed to expose AI-powered harms, including synthetic identity fraud, deepfake exploitation, and cyber-enabled extortion networks. We provide journalists, researchers, and investigators with data-driven insights to map and understand these emerging threats.

Who is Dark AI for?

Dark AI is designed for journalists, researchers, cybersecurity professionals, and anyone investigating AI-powered harms. Our tools help map infrastructure, track synthetic identities, analyze deepfake networks, and expose malicious actors operating in these spaces.

Is Dark AI free to use?

Yes, Dark AI is free to use. We believe that investigative tools for exposing AI harms should be accessible to journalists and researchers. Some advanced features may require API keys for third-party services, but the core functionality is available at no cost.

Tools

What is PersonaForge?

PersonaForge maps synthetic identity vendors and their infrastructure. It tracks domains, hosting providers, payment processors, and communication channels used by vendors selling fake IDs, passports, and other identity documents. The tool helps investigators understand the ecosystem of synthetic identity fraud.

What is ShadowStack?

ShadowStack tracks the infrastructure behind deepfake and "nudify" services. It monitors domains, DNS history, hosting providers, CDNs, and other technical infrastructure used by services that create non-consensual deepfake imagery. This helps researchers map the technical footprint of these operations.

What is BlackWire?

BlackWire provides threat intelligence and security analysis for domains and infrastructure. It aggregates data from multiple sources to provide comprehensive security assessments, helping investigators identify malicious infrastructure and understand threat patterns.

What is HumanGate?

HumanGate is a WordPress plugin designed to help website owners protect their content from AI scraping and deepfake generation. It provides technical controls to prevent automated systems from harvesting images and content for use in AI training or deepfake creation.

What is DFaceSearch?

DFaceSearch is a face recognition tool that helps identify when the same person appears across multiple deepfake or synthetic media. It uses privacy-preserving techniques to match faces without storing biometric data, helping investigators track the reuse of images across different platforms and services.

Data & Methodology

Where does the data come from?

Our data comes from multiple sources:

  • Public sources: WHOIS records, DNS data, SSL certificates, and other publicly available information.
  • API integrations: We integrate with various threat intelligence and domain analysis APIs to enrich our data.
  • Manual research: Our team conducts ongoing research to identify and catalog new vendors, services, and infrastructure.
  • Community contributions: Researchers and journalists can contribute findings through our contact form.

We prioritize publicly available data and do not engage in hacking, unauthorized access, or illegal data collection.

How often is the data updated?

Data updates vary by tool:

  • PersonaForge: Continuous monitoring with updates for new domains and vendor information as they are discovered.
  • ShadowStack: DNS history and infrastructure data updated as new services are discovered.
  • BlackWire: Threat intelligence data refreshed from multiple sources as new information becomes available.

Reports are updated when significant new findings emerge or as new data becomes available.

What technical terms should I know?

Here are some key terms used throughout our platform:

  • CDN (Content Delivery Network): A network of servers that deliver content to users based on geographic location, improving speed and performance.
  • ASN (Autonomous System Number): A unique identifier for a network or group of networks under a single administrative control.
  • Registrar: A company that manages domain name registrations (e.g., GoDaddy, Namecheap).
  • WHOIS: A protocol for querying databases that store information about domain names and IP addresses.
  • DNS History: Historical records of domain name system changes, showing how domains have been configured over time.
  • SSL/TLS Certificate: Digital certificates that enable encrypted connections between web servers and browsers.

Legal & Privacy

Is using Dark AI legal?

Yes, Dark AI only uses publicly available data and legitimate research methods. We do not engage in hacking, unauthorized access, or illegal data collection. All data sources are publicly accessible or obtained through legitimate API services. However, how you use the information is your responsibility—always ensure your research and reporting comply with applicable laws and ethical guidelines.

Does Dark AI store personal information?

Dark AI focuses on infrastructure and technical data (domains, IP addresses, hosting providers, etc.), not personal information about individuals. We do not collect or store personal data about users of our platform. Some tools, like DFaceSearch, use privacy-preserving techniques that do not store biometric data. We prioritize user privacy and data protection.

Can I report abuse or request data removal?

If you believe our platform contains inaccurate information or data that should be removed, please contact us through our contact form. We review all requests and will remove or correct data when appropriate. However, note that we primarily track infrastructure and technical data, not personal information about individuals.

Usage & Taking Action

How can journalists use Dark AI?

Journalists can use Dark AI to:

  • Map infrastructure behind synthetic identity vendors or deepfake services
  • Track domain changes and infrastructure shifts over time
  • Identify patterns in hosting providers, payment processors, and other technical services
  • Export data for further analysis and visualization
  • Use research reports as sources for investigative stories

Our tools are designed to support investigative reporting on AI-powered harms.

What should I do if I find someone I know in the data?

Dark AI tracks infrastructure and technical data, not personal information. If you discover infrastructure related to a service that has harmed you or someone you know, you may want to:

  • Document the findings for law enforcement or legal action
  • Report the service to relevant platforms (hosting providers, payment processors, etc.)
  • Contact support organizations that help victims of deepfake abuse
  • Reach out to us if you need assistance understanding the technical data

We are not a law enforcement agency, but we can help you understand the technical infrastructure data.

How can I contribute data or findings?

We welcome contributions from researchers and journalists. You can:

  • Report new vendors, services, or infrastructure through our contact form
  • Share research findings or patterns you've discovered
  • Suggest improvements to our tools or reports
  • Collaborate on research projects

All contributions help improve our understanding of AI-powered harms and make our tools more effective.
